## Summary
Gen Z Capital copies @evolving.ai's *engine* — one dramatic image per slide, the
handle + the STORY's PRODUCT logo on every slide, one idea per slide, source-cited
fact slides, "SWIPE FOR MORE" — but flips the *purpose*: where @evolving.ai
REPORTS spectacle, GZC TEACHES a usable skill and builds the "unfair AI advantage"
mindset (locked positioning: "Capital" = leverage, not money; audience = Gen Z who
feel behind on AI). **We launch with ONE type first: the TUTORIAL / how-to
carousel.** Its spine: cover hook = "outcome in X steps" (default; rotate "you're
doing it wrong" / "the trick nobody's using") → numbered STEP slides, each an
AI-generated cinematic image + a small source tag on any fact slide + one numbered
focal point → recap slide (re-lists steps = the SAVE engine) → CTA slide (names the
specific ongoing value of following; NEVER bare "follow for more", no link until
Phase 2). V1 is FULLY AUTOMATED with AI-generated images; real tool-UI screenshots
are deferred to a later phase. Cadence: reels and carousels post on DIFFERENT days
(reels = reach, carousels = saves), all at 8pm SGT (audience = Singapore + Malaysia,
UTC+8). This is the format we build and ship first.

## Goal
Decide which essential elements of @evolving.ai's carousel format Gen Z Capital
should ADOPT exactly, BEND, or DROP — so GZC becomes as successful as
@evolving.ai but on its own terms: teach a usable AI skill, no fluff/no hype,
dark cinematic + neon-green (#39FF14), faceless + automated. Also: which
content/slide TYPES GZC should run.

## Grounding — what we already know (from real @evolving.ai slides studied)
Studied 10 covers + one complete multi-slide post. Confirmed essentials:
- One dramatic image per slide, edge-to-edge (no text-only slides).
- Handle + the STORY's PRODUCT logo on every slide (consistency engine).
- One idea per slide; slide count flexes to the story (3–12 slides).
- Dark corner-slash frame + rounded media card + small numbered label.
- "SWIPE FOR MORE" on the cover.
- Source citations on data slides (e.g. `SOURCE: ARENA.AI`) = credibility.
- Real artifacts over stock (actual MySpace page, actual leaderboard screenshot).
- Slide TYPES they cycle: listicle item, data/leaderboard (cited), real-artifact
  screenshot, polished-result/campaign slide.

Where GZC must diverge: they REPORT, GZC TEACHES (every post ends in a usable
skill); white accent → neon-green; spectacle → saves.

## Key Highlights
- **Copy the engine, flip the purpose.** Same consistency machine as @evolving.ai
  (1 image/slide, handle + product logo every slide, 1 idea/slide, source-cited
  facts, "SWIPE FOR MORE") — but WE TEACH a skill where THEY REPORT news.
- **Product logo, not parent.** A Claude story shows the Claude starburst, not the
  Anthropic "A" (already shipped in source_logo.py). This is an @evolving.ai move.
- **The recap slide is the save engine** — for a how-to, re-listing the steps in
  one slide is the single biggest reason it gets bookmarked. Non-negotiable.
- **Hook on the viewer, never on AI spectacle** — "Build an AI app in 4 steps"
  beats "look what AI did." The viewer is the protagonist; the tool is the weapon.
- **v1 = fully automated AI images.** Real screenshots are a known upgrade for a
  later phase, not a launch blocker.
- **Different days for reels vs carousels**, both 8pm SGT (UTC+8 audience).
