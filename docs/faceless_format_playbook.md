Last updated: 2026-05-02. Synthesized from engagement-rate research across the
top 12 faceless accounts in the money / AI / mindset niches.

---

## TL;DR — What's working in 2026

The meta has split. **Carousels win saves**, **short cinematic reels win reach**.
The highest-engagement accounts run BOTH, not one or the other.

The dominant reel format in money/wealth/mindset: **cinematic stock B-roll +
one-line bold text overlay + male voiceover**. 12–22 seconds. Single hook frame.

Profile-screenshot montage (the format Reel #1 uses) is in measured decline.
Instagram's December 2025 Originality Score penalizes recycled screenshot
compilations — reach drops of 60–80% reported. Use it sparingly, not as the
primary format.

## Format spec — Primary cinematic-reel template

Build this as the default reel template for daily posting.

**Visual**

- 9:16, 1080×1920, 30 fps
- One slow-mo cinematic stock clip (Pexels/Pixabay/Mixkit) per beat
  - Beat 1 (0–3s): luxury, money, code, or wealth-adjacent loop
  - Beat 2 (3–10s): related second clip, contrasting tone
  - Beat 3 (10–20s): payoff clip — cinematic reveal
  - Beat 4 (20–27s): proof shot — close-up, single-subject
  - Beat 5 (27–30s): black background, CTA only
- Text overlay: 1 line per beat, Anton font, neon green or white
- Vignette + brightness 0.62 + contrast 1.12 (the user's existing style holds)
- Persistent corner logo (already built)

**Audio**

- Voiceover: OpenAI TTS `tts-1-hd`, voice `onyx` (deep male) — already wired
- Music: cinematic bass loop at 18% volume under VO (add later, optional)
- No SFX whooshes — they read amateur in 2026

**Pacing rules**

- Hook: visible text + first VO syllable in the first 0.5s
- No beat over 7s
- Hard cuts only; no fades

**Hook lines that work (test these first)**

- "Most people will never make a million dollars. Here's why."
- "Instagram doesn't count likes anymore. They count this."
- "Six accounts. 30 million followers. None of them showed their face."
- "AI just made this person $40,000 in 30 days."
- "If you have a phone and 20 minutes, you can build this."

## Algorithm signals to optimize for (2026)

Per Instagram (Mosseri, Dec 2025) and verified by Hootsuite + Sprout + Buffer
2026 algorithm guides, the three Reels ranking pillars in order:

1. **Watch time (relative %)** — minutes watched ÷ reel length. Want 70%+.
2. **Sends-per-reach** — DM shares ÷ unique viewers. **Want >1%** for viral push.
3. **Likes-per-reach** — likes ÷ unique viewers.

Saves are de-emphasized vs. 2023. Comments still count but lower weight than
sends. The single highest-leverage move for a 0-follower account: **write a
hook line that triggers "send this to someone who…"** — that drives sends.

## What to stop doing

- Long intros / setup before the hook — first 0.5s must reveal the angle
- Multiple text lines on screen at once — pick ONE per beat
- Whoosh SFX on cuts — reads amateur in 2026
- Profile-screenshot reels as primary format — Originality Score penalty
- Hashtag spam (28+ hashtags) — keep it to 8–15 niche-relevant tags
- Posting at the wrong local time — our audience is **Singapore + Malaysia
  (UTC+8)**; post **8pm SGT** (peak evening window). Reels and carousels go out
  on **different days** (reels = reach days, carousels = save days), never both
  the same day. (Supersedes the old "weekday evenings ET" guidance — that was the
  early US-audience assumption and no longer applies.)

## Weekly research workflow

Every Monday, spend 30 min:

1. Open the 12 model accounts above. Pick the top 3 by recent reel views.
2. Note the hook line, beat structure, and length of each.
3. Run one of those accounts through Inflact Profile Analyzer for ER + posting times.
4. Drop one new format finding into `assets/reel_scripts/` as a script seed.
5. Pick the next reel topic from `research/research.py` output (Google Sheet).

The goal is to ship 1 reel/day on the cinematic template, while the carousel
side runs through the existing `publisher/carousel_generator.py`.
