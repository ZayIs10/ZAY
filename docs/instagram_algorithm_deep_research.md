## 1. The 2026 Algorithm — What Actually Decides Reach

### 1.1 There is no "the algorithm" anymore
Mosseri publicly retired the singular term in 2025. Instagram now runs **separate ranking systems per surface**:
- **Feed** ranker (followers see this)
- **Stories** ranker
- **Reels** ranker (the discovery engine — non-followers see this)
- **Explore** ranker (cold-start recommendations)

Implication: a post that wins on one surface can flop on another. **Optimize per surface, not per "algorithm".**

### 1.2 Top 3 signals confirmed by Mosseri (Jan 2025, still in force May 2026)
In rank order across all surfaces:
1. **Watch time** — total seconds watched, weighted by completion %
2. **Sends per reach (DM shares)** — strongest "this is valuable" signal
3. **Likes per reach** — re-elevated in 2026 as a quality proxy (was demoted in 2024)

Saves and comments still matter, but they sit *below* these three.

### 1.3 What changed in the last 6 months
- **Likes per reach** got promoted back into the top 3 (was bottom-tier in 2024). Quality bar rose.
- **Public posts from professional accounts (18+) are now indexed by Google by default** since mid-2025. Captions = SEO that works *outside* Instagram too.
- **3-minute Reels** are eligible for Explore IF retention holds above ~60%.
- **Aggregator penalty** got harsher: any TikTok/CapCut watermark = automatic suppression, not just demotion.
- **Original audio** beats trending audio for accounts under 10K — original sounds get a small-account boost.

### 1.4 Engagement-rate benchmarks by tier (May 2026)
| Tier | Overall ER | Reels ER | Carousel ER | Static ER |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nano (1K–10K) | **9–15%** | 5–9% | 4–6% | 2–3% |
| Micro (10K–100K) | 4–7% | 3–5% | 2–3% | 1–2% |
| Mid (100K–500K) | 2–4% | 2–3% | 1.5–2% | 0.8–1.2% |

**GenZ Capital is in the nano tier.** This is structurally the easiest tier to grow from — Instagram trusts small accounts more because their engagement is harder to fake. Don't waste it on weak content.

## 2. The Viral Script Structure (Reels)

Battle-tested across 118K+ analyzed viral videos. Every viral Reel from a finance creator follows this:

```
[0–3s]   HOOK       Stop the scroll. Visual + verbal at the same time.
[3–15s]  PROBLEM    Name the pain or knowledge gap. Make it personal.
[15–45s] SOLUTION   Deliver the payoff. One core idea, no tangents.
[45–55s] CTA        Save / send to a friend / follow for more.
```

**Targets:**
- Watch-through rate **>60%** (algorithmic boost threshold)
- Completion rate **>70%** (viral boost)
- Sends per reach **>1%** (distribution multiplier)

### 2.1 Length sweet spots
| Goal | Length | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Top-of-funnel discovery | **7–15s** | Highest completion %, easy looping |
| Educational / save-worthy | 30–60s | Enough payload to be saveable |
| Deep-dive (eligible for Explore) | up to 3 min | Only if retention holds >60% |

**Default for GenZ Capital Reels: 12–20 seconds.** Long enough to deliver one stat + one consequence, short enough to loop.

### 2.2 The 4-beat opening (frame-by-frame)
- **Frame 0 (still):** Bold text overlay + striking image. ~50% of viewers watch muted — the hook *must* land visually.
- **Sec 0.5:** Voiceover hook starts. No "hey guys" — go straight in.
- **Sec 1.5:** Pattern interrupt (cut, zoom, color shift) — keeps the scroll-trigger from firing.
- **Sec 3:** Stake declared. Viewer knows what they'll get if they keep watching.

## 3. Hook Templates (Copy-Paste, Finance-Tuned)

These are the formulas that hold viewers past the 3-second cliff. Each one is mapped to a psychological trigger.

### 3.1 Curiosity gap (information asymmetry)
- "Most 20-somethings don't know this about [topic]…"
- "There's a reason your bank doesn't tell you about [X]."
- "If you make under $[X]/year, this changes everything."

### 3.2 Pain point
- "[Common habit] is killing your [outcome they want]."
- "You're losing $[X] a year and don't even know it."
- "The reason you can't save money is not what you think."

### 3.3 Contrarian / pattern break
- "Everyone says do X. Here's why that's broken."
- "Stop investing in [X]. Do this instead."
- "The advice your parents gave you about money is wrong."

### 3.4 Specificity / shock stat
- "I made $[exact number] in 90 days doing nothing but this."
- "73% of Gen Z don't know what an HSA is. Here's what you're missing."
- "$1 invested in [X] in 2020 is worth $[Y] today."

### 3.5 Social proof / authority
- "I work in private equity. Here's what we don't tell retail investors."
- "Every millionaire I know does this one thing."
- "After analyzing 500 portfolios, this is the #1 mistake."

### 3.6 Time-bound / FOMO
- "You have until [date] to do this or you lose [outcome]."
- "If you're under 25, you have an advantage you'll never get back."

**Rule for the pipeline:** every Reel and image post must use ONE of these 6 hook patterns. Reject any draft that opens with context, intro, or "today we're going to talk about…"

## 4. The Three Surfaces — Format Strategy

Each format has a different job. Don't pick one — run all three in rotation.

### 4.1 Reels = the GROWTH engine
- Algorithm pushes Reels to non-followers (Explore + Reels feed)
- Nano-tier Reels can reach **5–20× the impressions** of static posts
- Optimize for: watch time, sends, completion %
- Cadence: **3–5 Reels/week minimum**

### 4.2 Carousels = the ENGAGEMENT engine
- Highest **save rate** of any format (saves are an outsized ranking signal)
- Average engagement rate **0.55%** vs single image **0.41%**
- Algorithm gives carousels a "second pass": users who didn't engage on slide 1 see slide 2 reshown — extra reach you can't get from single images
- **Optimal slide count: 8–10 slides.** Anything under 4 slides barely outperforms a single image.
- Optimize for: saves per reach, swipe-through %, dwell time
- Cadence: **2–3 carousels/week**

### 4.3 Stories = the LOYALTY engine
- Doesn't drive new-follower reach but feeds the "close connection" graph
- Polls, sliders, question stickers all rank higher than passive views
- Cadence: **2–5 stories/day**

### 4.4 Static posts = mostly skip
Static (single image) posts are the lowest-leverage format in 2026. Use them only when:
- The asset is too detailed for a carousel split
- You need a quick portfolio piece
- You're A/B testing copy

The current GenZ Capital pipeline is mostly static (1080×1350). **Biggest single improvement: move 60% of output to carousels, 30% to Reels, 10% to static.**

## 5. Carousel Playbook (Highest-Leverage Quick Win for This Project)

The pipeline already has [carousel_generator.py](publisher/carousel_generator.py). Here's how to make it actually convert.

### 5.1 The carousel architecture
| Slide | Job | Example (finance) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Hook + promise** | "5 money moves nobody told you about in your 20s" |
| 2 | Reframe / agitate | "The reason most people stay broke isn't income — it's this" |
| 3–8 | One idea per slide | Each slide = one mini-payoff |
| 9 | The biggest insight (saved for late) | "And the one nobody talks about…" |
| 10 | CTA + save prompt | "Save this for your future self. Send to the friend who needs it." |

### 5.2 Visual rules
- **Slide 1 = the entire post's job.** If slide 1 doesn't hook, no one swipes. Treat it like a Reel cover.
- **Cliffhanger between slides:** every slide must end with a reason to swipe (incomplete sentence, "but here's the kicker…", arrow pointing right).
- **Same visual identity across slides** — algorithm rewards cohesive design (signals "real creator, not spam").
- **Slide 10 always asks for the save** — explicit > implicit. "Save this for your future self" outperforms "thanks for reading."

### 5.3 What to feed Claude in the generator prompt
For each carousel topic, generate:
1. Hook headline (slide 1) — pick from the 6 hook patterns in §3
2. 8 distinct insights (one per middle slide)
3. Cliffhanger transitions between slides
4. Big-finish insight (slide 9)
5. CTA + save prompt (slide 10)

## 6. Caption SEO — The Underused Lever

Hashtags are mostly dead as a discovery mechanism. **Caption keywords now outrank hashtags.** Public professional posts also get indexed by Google.

### 6.1 Caption structure
```
[Hook line — under 125 chars, repeats the topic keyword]

[Body — 2-4 short paragraphs, natural keyword use, no keyword stuffing]

[Save/share CTA]

[3-5 niche hashtags, max]
```

### 6.2 The 125-character rule
Instagram cuts off captions at ~125 characters with a "more" button. **Anything after that is invisible unless they tap.** The hook must fit there AND repeat the search keyword.

Bad: "Hey guys! Today we're going to talk about something really important about money…"
Good: "Compound interest is the #1 reason your 20s decide your 50s. Here's the math nobody shows you →"

### 6.3 Long-tail keywords for finance/Gen Z
Search-optimize for what people actually type into Instagram search:
- "how to invest in your 20s"
- "money mistakes Gen Z"
- "how to save money fast"
- "passive income for beginners"
- "Roth IRA explained"
- "credit score under 25"
- "first job money tips"
- "side hustle ideas 2026"

**Rule for the pipeline:** every caption must contain at least 2 of these phrases naturally — front-load the most relevant one in the first 125 characters.

### 6.4 Alt text matters now
Alt text is a top-tier ranking signal in 2026 (used to be just for accessibility). The pipeline should auto-write alt text describing the visual + topic — currently this is unset on most posts.

### 6.5 Hashtag strategy (small account)
Mix **3 sizes, 5 total**:
- 1 broad (1M+ posts) — e.g. `#personalfinance`
- 2–3 medium (10K–500K posts) — e.g. `#genzfinance`, `#moneytipsforyou`
- 1–2 small niche (under 10K posts) — easier to rank "top posts" on these

Place hashtags at the **end of the caption** or in the **first comment** (both work equally well in 2026 — first comment is no longer penalized).

## 7. Finance + Gen Z Niche — What's Actually Working in May 2026

### 7.1 Why finance is a goldmine here
- **3–5× higher CPM than lifestyle content** (Influencer Marketing Hub 2026 report)
- **68% of Gen Z have tried a financial trend they saw online** (Credit Karma study)
- **34% of Gen Z get money advice from Instagram** (second only to TikTok at 39%)
- Polished, educational finance content gets saved at premium rates → algorithmic flywheel

### 7.2 Currently viral financial trends to ride
- **Soft saving** — saving while still spending on experiences (anti-FIRE backlash)
- **Loud budgeting** — public, vocal frugality ("I can't go out, I'm saving for X")
- **Tax-smart spending** — ITM accounts, HSAs, 529s for non-parents
- **The "compound interest visualization"** — every age-vs-portfolio chart still goes viral
- **"What I make / what I keep"** breakdown of paychecks

### 7.3 Top-performing format archetypes (finance niche)
1. **"How I earn while I sleep"** — passive income reveals
2. **"Save this financial checklist"** — utility carousels (highest save rate)
3. **"Money mistakes in my 20s"** — reflection / regret stories
4. **"$X to $Y in N months"** — transformation timelines with proof
5. **"Things rich people do that broke people don't"** — contrast posts (high share rate)
6. **"What [job role] doesn't tell you about your money"** — insider authority

### 7.4 Tone calibration
The brand voice rules in `CLAUDE.md` (direct, serious, no hype, no emojis, dark cinematic) are *correct* for the algorithm — they map to **authority + curiosity** triggers, which outperform hype + excitement triggers in finance content. Don't water this down.

What to add: **specificity.** Generic "invest early" content gets average engagement. "$200/month from age 22 = $1.4M at 65" gets sent. **Numbers beat adjectives.**

## 8. Posting Cadence + Best Times (May 2026)

### 8.1 The right cadence (data from Buffer's 2M-post study)
| Frequency | Outcome |
|---|---|
| 0 posts/week | Account stagnates or loses followers |
| 1–2 posts/week | Maintenance, no growth |
| **3–5 posts/week** | **Growth zone — biggest jump** |
| 6–7 posts/week | Diminishing returns, only worthwhile if quality holds |

GenZ Capital is currently at **1 post/day = 7/week**. That's fine *if* quality holds. Drop to 5/week if quality slips.

### 8.2 The format mix
For a nano-tier finance account:
- **3 Reels/week**
- **2–3 carousels/week**
- **1 static/week** (max)
- **Daily Stories**

### 8.3 Best times to post (US-tuned, May 2026 data)
**Top 3 windows globally (from 9.6M post study):**
1. **Thursday 9pm local**
2. **Sunday 8–10pm local**
3. **Wednesday 8–10pm local**

**Other strong windows:**
- Mon 2–4pm
- Tue 1–7pm
- Wed 12pm–9pm
- **5am audience-local time** — sleeper hit; low competition, catches the morning scroll

For a Gen Z finance audience (US, college + early-career): **Sun 8pm and Wed/Thu 9pm** are the highest-leverage slots.

### 8.4 Spacing rule
**Never post 2 Reels within 6 hours.** Instagram suppresses the second one to avoid spamming followers. One prime-time slot per day, period.

## 9. Small-Account Survival Rules (Under 10K)

This is GenZ Capital's tier. Specific tactics that compound at this size:

1. **Reply to every comment in the first hour.** Boosts the post's "interaction velocity" score — Instagram uses this to decide whether to push the post wider.
2. **DM every new follower within 24h.** Builds the close-connection graph that powers Story + Feed reach.
3. **Niche down hard.** Every post must clearly belong to ONE topic. Algorithm classifier needs ~30 days of consistent topic to lock in your account profile. **If more than 30% of your last 20 posts fall outside the core niche, the algorithm cannot stably recommend you.**
4. **No follow/unfollow.** Flagged behavior, suppresses account-wide for 7+ days.
5. **No engagement bait.** "Comment YES if…", "follow for more" → demotion.
6. **Original audio when possible.** Trending audio is fine but original gives nano accounts a small boost.
7. **Don't delete posts.** Triggers a "deleter" flag. Archive instead — invisible to followers, no flag.

## 10. The 3-Metric Scorecard (Use on Every Post After 48h)

| Metric | Floor (archive) | Good | Viral signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sends per reach | <0.5% | 1% | **>2%** |
| Avg watch time (Reels) | <40% | 60% | **>85%** |
| Saves per reach | <0.3% | 0.8% | **>1.5%** |

**Decision rule:** If a post hits **2 of 3 viral signals** → make 5 more like it that week. If a post hits **0 of 3 floors** → archive it.

This is the single most important loop the pipeline is missing. Everything else (more Reels, better captions, etc.) is downstream of "do we measure what works." Until insights are pulled back automatically, every optimization is a guess.

## 11. Action List for the GenZ Capital Pipeline

Re-ranked from [instagram_growth_framework.md](instagram_growth_framework.md) priority list, with implementation pointers.

### Tier 1 — unblocks everything else
1. **48-hour insights pull-back.** After every post, call Meta Graph `/insights` 48h later → write `sends_per_reach`, `avg_watch_time`, `saves_per_reach` to the same Google Sheet row that triggered the post. Without this metric loop, all other changes are unmeasured.
2. **Reels generator.** The pipeline is image-only. The single biggest leverage is moving 40% of output to Reels. Existing [reel_generator.py](publisher/reel_generator.py) and [publish_reel.py](publisher/publish_reel.py) are already in the repo — finish them.

### Tier 2 — content quality
3. **Hook enforcement in [post_generator.py](publisher/post_generator.py).** Every generated headline must match one of the 6 hook patterns in §3. Reject + regenerate if it opens with context, intro, or "today we're talking about…"
4. **Caption SEO upgrade.** Caption first 125 chars must include the topic keyword. Add 2 long-tail finance keywords to the body. Auto-write alt text. 3–5 hashtags max.
5. **Carousel-first refactor.** Bias [carousel_generator.py](publisher/carousel_generator.py) to 8–10 slides with cliffhangers between slides + explicit save CTA on slide 10.

### Tier 3 — feedback loops
6. **Apify competitor scraper.** 10 finance/Gen Z accounts (50K–2M) → top 30 posts → ranked by saves+sends, NOT likes.
7. **Apify self-scraper + weekly "what's working" Claude report.** Reads own performance + competitor performance → weekly brief.
8. **Comment auto-reply (first hour).** Boosts interaction velocity score. Even a generic "💯" beats silence in the first 60 minutes.

### Not blocking growth
- Notion vs Sheets is a substitution, not a missing feature
- Monetization (clipping, products, shoutouts) is locked behind 10K — don't optimize for it yet
