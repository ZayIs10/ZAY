## 1. The one-line positioning

> **GenZ Capital = your unfair AI advantage.**
> We find the AI tricks that actually move the needle, so you don't have to.

The word **"Capital" is reframed**: it does NOT mean money/finance anymore.
It means **leverage** — the capital that matters now is knowing how to use AI
better than everyone around you. We are building that capital for the viewer,
one reel at a time.

This reframe is deliberate: it keeps the brand equity already built into the
name, the logo, and the neon aesthetic, while pointing it at the AI-tools niche
the channel pivoted to (see [niche_pivot]).

---

## 2. What we actually sell (the promise)

We are **not** a "look how cool AI is" channel. We are a **leverage** channel.
Every piece of content must pass this test:

> After watching, can the viewer DO something today they couldn't do 30 seconds ago?

If no → it's edutainment, not leverage → it does not get made.

The promise to the viewer, in their words:
- "This account finds the AI stuff that's actually worth my time."
- "I don't have to scroll 50 videos — they hand me the one trick that works."
- "Following them is how I stay ahead without doing the research myself."

That last line is the **entire reason to follow** — and therefore the spine of
every follow-CTA we write.

## 3. Who it's for

- **Primary:** Gen Z + young millennials who feel they're "behind" on AI and
  don't want to be. Ambitious, time-poor, scroll-native.
- **Mindset we speak to:** FOMO + ambition. "Everyone's using AI to get ahead
  and I'm not — fix that for me."
- **NOT for:** AI researchers, prompt-engineering purists, people who want
  theory. We are applied, fast, and a little aggressive.

---

## 4. Voice (unchanged from the reel format — restated for CTAs)

- Direct, high-stakes, no fluff, no emojis, no hype words ("amazing!", "insane!").
- Neon-green energy: confident, slightly provocative, never cringe.
- We talk like the friend who's already ahead and is letting you in on it —
  not a teacher, not a hype-man.
- Second person ("you", "your") always. The viewer is the protagonist; the AI
  tool is the weapon we're handing them.

## 5. The name in practice (how "GenZ Capital" reads on a reel)

- **Persistent logo** (bottom-right, per the reel format spec) = the signature.
  It is NOT a CTA. It just brands the frame.
- **Spoken/handle CTA** appears only in Beat 6. The name is framed as the
  *source of leverage*, e.g. "— GenZ Capital. I find these so you don't have to."
- Never explain the name on a reel. Never say "capital means leverage" out loud.
  The content proves it; we don't argue it.

## 6. Platform reality (drives CTA phrasing — READ before writing any CTA)

Reels are cross-posted to **YouTube Shorts + Instagram Reels** (confirmed
2026-06-18). The two platforms have *opposite* tolerance for follow-CTAs:

| | YouTube Shorts | Instagram Reels |
|---|---|---|
| "Subscribe / follow for more" | Encouraged | **Suppressed as engagement bait** (see [instagram_growth_framework.md] §0.8) |
| What actually drives reach | Watch %, subs, "next video" | Sends-per-reach, watch %/replays, saves |

**The rule that satisfies both:** a CTA must drive a follow **by naming the
specific ongoing value of following**, never with the bare phrase "follow for
more." Specific > generic — and specific is also simply a better CTA.

✅ "Follow — I find one of these every day so you don't have to."
✅ "This is part 3. The rest live on the page."
❌ "Follow for more!" (IG bait flag + weak everywhere)
❌ "Like and subscribe!" (lazy, platform-mismatched)

See [genz_cta_playbook.md](genz_cta_playbook.md) for the full CTA system that
implements this.

## 7. The phased CTA strategy (the big strategic call)

This is the answer to "should every reel push my link?" — **No, not yet.**

**Phase 1 — NOW (until a destination exists):** every reel's Beat-6 CTA drives
**FOLLOW or LOOP**. No "link in description." Rationale: with a small audience
and nothing built to send people to, a link CTA leaks attention and converts
~nobody, while costing the watch-time/follow signals that actually grow the
channel. Build the audience first.

**Phase 2 — LATER (trigger: a real destination ships):** once *either* a long-
form YouTube video library *or* a freebie/lead-magnet exists, ~1 in 4 reels
switches its Beat-6 CTA to point there. The pipeline should treat this as a
**flag to flip**, not a rewrite.

Phase-2 trigger checklist (flip the flag when ANY is true):
- [ ] ≥3 long-form YouTube videos published worth sending Shorts traffic to
- [ ] A freebie exists and is hosted (PDF / prompt pack / template / Notion)
- [ ] An owned channel exists to capture into (email list / Skool / Discord)

Until one of those is checked, Phase 1 stands. Do not add link CTAs early.

## 8. Do / Don't summary

**Do**
- Frame every win as *leverage the viewer now has*.
- Make following feel like subscribing to an *unfair advantage*, not "more content."
- Keep the name; let the content prove "Capital = leverage."

**Don't**
- Revert to money/finance framing (the pivot is permanent — see [niche_pivot]).
- Use "follow for more" / "like and subscribe" (bait + weak).
- Push a link CTA before Phase 2 triggers.
- Explain or defend the brand name on-screen.
